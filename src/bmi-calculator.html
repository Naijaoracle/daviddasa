Understanding Your BMI

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple yet effective tool to assess whether your weight is within a healthy range relative to your height. Knowing your BMI can provide valuable insights into your overall health and potential risks associated with your weight.

Here's what your BMI classification indicates:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.999
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.999
  • Obese: BMI 30 or greater

Understanding your BMI can serve as an initial assessment of your body weight and its potential impact on your health. While BMI doesn't directly measure body fat, it can be a useful screening tool. However, it's essential to note that individual health is complex, and other factors such as muscle mass and distribution may influence these classifications.

It's advisable to consult with a healthcare professional for a comprehensive assessment of your health, taking into account various factors beyond BMI.

Use the BMI calculator above to find your BMI and gain insights into your weight status. Remember that maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet and regular physical activity, contributes significantly to overall well-being.
