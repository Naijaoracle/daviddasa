Study overview

About the study

We are evaluating AI-driven healthcare training simulations delivered in Nigeria using VR and mobile devices. Your feedback helps us test educational quality, safety signalling, and usability in low-infrastructure settings.

Core scientific aim

Can AI-driven healthcare training simulations maintain educational quality, safety signalling, and usability when delivered under constrained infrastructure and across lower-cost devices?

To answer this, the trip operationalises two tightly controlled comparative studies conducted during a single visit.

Eligibility

  • Year 2+ students enrolled in a healthcare-related programme
  • Able to use a virtual reality headset and a smartphone

What's involved

  • One supervised simulation session using AI-supported clinical training technology
  • Delivered using a virtual reality headset and/or a mobile phone
  • Completion of short feedback questionnaires immediately after the session
  • Optional follow-up questionnaire approximately three weeks later

Study structure on the ground

The field trip contains two linked studies, referred to as Study A and Study B, delivered to healthcare students only.

Study A

VR headset with AI simulation versus VR headset without AI reasoning.

  • Same headset, same scenario, same interface
  • Only difference is the presence or absence of cloud-based LLM-driven reasoning
  • Tests robustness under poor or absent connectivity
  • Treats connectivity as a graded operational variable rather than a binary requirement
This study asks whether an offline or non-LLM fallback can be non-inferior for training quality.

Study B

VR headset with AI simulation versus mobile phone with AI simulation.

  • Same AI logic and safety constraints
  • Same clinical scenario and assessment framework
  • Different device class and fidelity level
This study asks whether a mobile-first AI simulation can deliver comparable value to immersive VR in an LMIC educational context.
Both studies use a within-participant crossover design to control for learner variability.
