Synthetic Data Principles and Disclosure Statement

Synthetic Data Research Artefact
Synthetic Data Ethics Governance

1. Core Principles

Synthetic-Only Mandate

All data used within this research framework is 100% synthetically generated. No real-world patient data (identifiable or de-identified) is used at any stage of the modelling or evaluation process. This ensures absolute privacy and eliminates the risk of re-identification.

Provenance Statement

Synthetic datasets are derived from high-level clinical guidelines, literature-based prevalence rates, and expert-defined physiological assumptions. The data does not originate from existing clinical databases or electronic health records (EHR).

Non-Clinical Use Boundary

These datasets are designed exclusively for research, simulation, and educational purposes. They must never be used for:

  • Clinical decision-making for real patients.
  • Training production-level diagnostic algorithms intended for clinical use.
  • Validation of safety-critical medical devices in a regulatory context.

2. System Separation

Logical Air-Gap

The synthetic data generation environment is logically and physically separate from any live clinical systems or hospital networks. There are no automated pipelines or data bridges between research environments and clinical operational environments.

Labelling and Metadata

Every record, file, and dashboard generated using this framework must be clearly labelled as "SYNTHETIC DATA - NOT FOR CLINICAL USE". This metadata must persist across all downstream research artefacts and visualisations.

3. Disclosure of Limitations

Fidelity Limits

While efforts are made to ensure clinical face validity (i.e., the data "looks" right to an expert), the synthetic data may not capture rare edge cases, complex multi-morbidities, or subtle physiological interactions not explicitly modelled in the assumptions register.

Bias Acknowledgement

Synthetic data reflects the assumptions of its designers. Bias may be present if the underlying literature or expert guidance used to define the generation rules contains demographic or clinical biases.
