Standards Alignment Narrative

Synthetic Data Research Artefact
Synthetic Data Standards Interoperability

Purpose

This document provides a conceptual mapping between the synthetic data concepts in this project and common healthcare data standards. It is descriptive and non-computable, and does not include schemas or profiles.

Conceptual Alignment (Narrative)

Patient and Person Concepts

  • The synthetic "Patient Profile" aligns with the idea of a patient or person record in standards such as FHIR and openEHR.
  • Demographic attributes are described at the level of categories (age range, gender identity), not as structured fields.

Encounter and Episode Concepts

  • The "Clinical Encounter" mirrors a visit or encounter concept.
  • The intent is to capture a moment-in-time interaction, not a full longitudinal record.

Observations and Measurements

  • "Vital Signs" and "Observations" reflect standard observation concepts.
  • Values are presented as narrative examples, avoiding codified terminology or LOINC mappings.

Medication and Intervention Concepts

  • "Medication Events" conceptually align with medication administration or prescription records.
  • Drug classes are described in generic terms without codes or structured drug dictionaries.

Alignment Boundaries

  • No conformance resources, profiles, or computable mappings are provided.
  • This narrative is intended for human readers to understand conceptual overlap only.

Intended Use

This alignment narrative supports transparency and interdisciplinary communication while preserving the non-operational boundary of the research artefacts.
