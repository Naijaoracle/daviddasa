Fidelity and Validity Plan

Digital Twin Research Artefact
Digital Twin Validation Fidelity

Purpose

This plan outlines how synthetic simulations will be evaluated for face validity and conceptual alignment with clinical expectations. It establishes the philosophical and methodological foundation for assessing whether XR-NPC systems produce outputs that are sufficiently realistic and educationally valuable for healthcare training contexts.

The fidelity-validity framework addresses a fundamental challenge in simulation-based education: how realistic does a simulation need to be to achieve its learning objectives? This plan rejects the assumption that maximum fidelity is always desirable, instead advocating for functional fidelity — simulation accuracy calibrated to specific educational goals.

Key Distinction: Fidelity vs. Validity

Fidelity

The degree to which the simulation replicates real-world characteristics — physical, functional, and psychological realism.

Validity

The degree to which the simulation measures or achieves what it claims to — whether it actually supports intended learning outcomes.

High fidelity without validity is wasted effort. Low fidelity with high validity may be optimal for certain training objectives.

Limitations and Boundaries

  • This plan does not validate against real-world clinical outcomes.
  • It does not provide parameter fitting, calibration procedures, or executable validation tools.
  • Validation is qualitative and expert-driven, not statistical or automated.
  • Results apply only to synthetic, non-clinical research contexts.

Validity Framework

The validation approach draws on established frameworks for simulation validity while adapting them for AI-driven NPC evaluation. Three complementary validity dimensions are assessed:

1. Construct Validity

Assesses whether the simulation accurately represents the theoretical constructs it claims to model — clinical reasoning patterns, physiological responses, and workflow dynamics.

TargetValidation QuestionAssessment Method
Clinical reasoning representation Do decision pathways reflect commonly taught clinical reasoning steps? Expert panel review against clinical guidelines and educational frameworks.
Physiological coherence Do simulated patient states follow plausible physiological trajectories? Clinical expert assessment of vital sign patterns and progression timing.
Role behaviour accuracy Do NPC behaviours reflect realistic professional role boundaries? Practitioner feedback on role-appropriate communication and actions.
Uncertainty representation Does the simulation appropriately model clinical ambiguity and incomplete information? Expert assessment of information availability and diagnostic uncertainty handling.

2. Face Validity

Evaluates whether the simulation "looks right" to domain experts — the intuitive assessment of realism and plausibility.

Assessment FocusQuestions for ExpertsEvidence Collected
Narrative believability Does this scenario feel like something that could happen in clinical practice? Likert ratings, free-text comments on plausibility gaps.
Character authenticity Do the NPCs behave like real colleagues, patients, or family members? Role-specific authenticity ratings, behavioural inconsistency notes.
Environmental coherence Does the clinical environment feel realistic for the scenario context? Setting appropriateness ratings, resource availability feedback.
Dialogue naturalness Does the communication feel natural and professionally appropriate? Communication quality ratings, awkward exchange identification.

3. Content Validity

Examines whether the simulation covers the appropriate range of clinical content for its educational objectives.

DimensionAssessment QuestionCoverage Analysis
Clinical breadth Does the scenario address the intended clinical competencies? Mapping to learning objectives and competency frameworks.
Decision complexity Does the scenario present appropriate decision-making challenges? Decision point analysis, cognitive load assessment.
Edge case coverage Does the scenario include appropriate exceptions and complications? Complication inventory, exception handling review.

Validation Process and Sensitivity Analysis

Expert Review Process

Validation is conducted through structured expert review panels following a defined protocol:

  1. Panel composition: Minimum 3-5 experts with relevant clinical and/or simulation expertise. Include both domain specialists and simulation education experts.
  2. Review materials: Experts receive scenario narratives, NPC behaviour descriptions, and sample outputs without technical implementation details.
  3. Independent assessment: Each expert completes validity assessments independently before group discussion.
  4. Consensus building: Group discussion focuses on disagreements and identifies specific revision targets.
  5. Iterative refinement: Scenarios are revised based on feedback, with changes documented and re-reviewed.

Sensitivity Analysis Approach

To understand how robust simulation validity is to variation, scenarios undergo conceptual sensitivity analysis:

Information Availability

Vary what clinical information is available to the learner. Does the scenario remain plausible when key data is delayed or incomplete?

  • Baseline: Full information available
  • Variant A: Key lab results delayed
  • Variant B: Patient history incomplete

Time Pressure

Vary the urgency of the clinical context. Do NPC behaviours remain appropriate under different temporal constraints?

  • Baseline: Standard pace
  • Variant A: Rapid deterioration
  • Variant B: Extended observation period

Resource Constraints

Vary available resources (staff, equipment, beds). Do workflow patterns adapt appropriately?

  • Baseline: Adequate resources
  • Variant A: Staff shortage
  • Variant B: Equipment unavailable

Patient Complexity

Vary comorbidities and social factors. Do clinical reasoning patterns remain coherent with added complexity?

  • Baseline: Single presenting problem
  • Variant A: Multiple comorbidities
  • Variant B: Complex social context

Acceptance Criteria

Scenarios are considered validated when:

  • Construct validity: Expert panel reaches consensus that core clinical constructs are appropriately represented.
  • Face validity: Majority of experts rate overall plausibility as "acceptable" or higher without major concerns.
  • Content validity: Learning objectives are demonstrably addressable through the scenario content.
  • Sensitivity robustness: Scenario remains plausible under at least 2 of 4 sensitivity variations.
