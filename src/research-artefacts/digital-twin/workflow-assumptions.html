Operational Workflow Modelling Assumptions Register

Digital Twin Research Artefact
Digital Twin Workflow Clinical Processes

Purpose

This register captures qualitative assumptions about operational workflows in clinical scenarios. It avoids executable simulation logic.

Workflow Assumptions

Queues and Flow

  • Patient flow is described in stages (arrival, triage, assessment, disposition).
  • Queue pressure is described qualitatively (low, moderate, high).

Staffing and Roles

  • Staffing levels are described relative to demand (adequate, stretched, insufficient).
  • Roles are described by responsibility rather than shift schedules.

Handoffs and Escalation

  • Handoffs are narrative events that may introduce delays or information loss.
  • Escalation occurs when risk is assessed as rising or uncertainty increases.

Interruptions and Time Pressure

  • Interruptions are described as contextual stressors affecting decision quality.
  • Time pressure is described as "routine," "compressed," or "urgent."

Boundaries

  • No discrete event simulation logic is included.
  • No reusable libraries or state transition diagrams are provided.
